Choosing a support platform means weighing setup time, pricing model, and how deeply each product actually understands your content. Here's how the three compare.

## Setup time

ContextGPT is built around pointing the AI at existing content — docs, websites, files — and getting a working chatbot in minutes. Intercom and Drift both offer AI features, but typically require more manual configuration of flows and knowledge base structure first.

## Pricing model

| Platform | Pricing basis | Best for |
|---|---|---|
| ContextGPT | Usage-based | Teams wanting fast setup, content-grounded answers |
| Intercom | Seat + resolution-based | Larger support orgs already on Intercom's suite |
| Drift | Seat-based | Sales-led orgs prioritizing conversational marketing |

## Answer grounding

ContextGPT is designed to cite the source content behind every answer, which matters for trust and for catching content gaps. Intercom and Drift both support knowledge-base-grounded responses, but the citation and gap-detection tooling varies by plan tier.

> [!NOTE]
> Pricing and feature tiers change frequently across all three platforms — always check current documentation before making a purchasing decision.

## Bottom line

If you want the fastest path from "existing content" to "working AI chatbot," ContextGPT is built specifically for that use case. Intercom and Drift are strong choices if you're already standardized on their broader support or sales suite.
