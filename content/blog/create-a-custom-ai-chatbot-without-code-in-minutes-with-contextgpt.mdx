## So, what is a Custom AI Chatbot?

<Paragraph>
A custom AI chatbot is an AI-powered assistant embedded on your website that answers questions using knowledge from *your* content — your pages, your documents, your product specs — instead of generic, one-size-fits-all responses.
</Paragraph>

<Paragraph>
The "custom" part is what makes it useful. Rather than a rigid, rule-based bot that only handles a handful of pre-scripted questions, a custom AI chatbot is trained on your business's actual knowledge base. It understands your product, your terminology, and your customers' most common questions, and responds accordingly — in your voice, with your facts.
</Paragraph>

<Paragraph>
Once trained, it can handle a wide range of jobs beyond simple Q&A:
</Paragraph>

- Answering customer support questions instantly, 24/7
- Qualifying and guiding sales leads before they talk to a human
- Collecting feedback or running lightweight surveys
- Pointing users to the right documentation or pricing page
- Integrating with your existing tools and workflows

<Paragraph>
Under the hood, it's powered by natural language processing to understand
 what a user is actually asking, combined with retrieval over your trained 
 content so answers stay grounded in facts you provided — not hallucinated.
  And because you don't need to write or maintain rule trees by hand, 
  non-technical teams can launch and update it themselves.
</Paragraph>

<Paragraph>
But wait, there's more! A custom AI chatbot is not just a one-trick pony.
 It's a multi-talented genius that can handle a wide range of tasks. Need 
 help with customer support? No problem, it can handle that with ease. 
 Want to automate your sales process? It's got your back. Need to collect
  feedback or conduct surveys? You got it! It can even integrate with other 
  systems and platforms to provide a seamless experience for your users.
</Paragraph>

<Paragraph>

underneath. It uses natural language processing to interpret what a user
 actually means rather than just matching keywords, and machine learning

### How to Build Your Chatbot in Seconds with ContextGPT: Written Instructions


#### Step 1: Pick your data source

- To create a new chatbot, click **"Create New Chatbot."** You'll then be
 able to choose from multiple data sources to provide the knowledge your
  chatbot.


#### Step 2: Import from a website URL

- Click on **"Import"** and select **"Website URL."**
- Copy your website link and paste it into the box, and click **"Fetch Links"**
- Review the fetched links and select the ones you want to include in your chatbot's knowledgebase.

#### Step 3: Import a single page

- If you only want specific information from a single page, select "Single Link."
- Paste the link into the designated area and click "Fetch Links."

#### Step 4: Import via sitemap

- If your site has a sitemap (usually at `yourdomain.com/sitemap.xml`), enter the URL.
- Click **"Fetch Links"** to let ContextGPT automatically discover and 
    import all your pages.

#### Step 5: Training the Chatbot

- Once selecting the data sources, you can train the chatbot in the gathered information
- Click "Next" to proceed with the training stage

#### Step 6: Adding Information About you Bot

- Enter your chatbot's name, welcome message, placeholder text, and other aesthetic details.
- Click "Create Chatbot" to finalize the chatbot's creation
- refresh the page, you will get your updated chatbot floating widget everywhere whether on you website embedded or your contextgpt dashboard

#### Step 7: Keep feeding it new info

- Editing doesn’t stop at creation. Add more links, files, or text anytime to make your chatbot smarter.
- You can add links, files, or custom text content to improve the chatbot's capabilities.

#### Step 8: Retrain when things change

- To test your chatbot, you can ask it questions related to the knowledge you've provided.
- The chatbot should be able to provide answers based on the information you've trained it on.

#### Step 9: Test it out

Before letting everyone talk to your chatbot, test it yourself. Pretend you're a customer and ask it all sorts of questions—even tricky ones! Make sure it gives correct, helpful answers and doesn't make things up.

### Step 10: Fine-tune the settings

Once the basics are working, explore the advanced settings. You can adjust its tone of voice, setup how it passes complex chats to a human teammate, change colors to match your brand, and hook it up to other apps.

> [NOTE]
> Treat your chatbot like a pet or a plant—it needs a little care and updating now and then to stay in top shape!

> [NOTE]
> You can see the content fetched from your website, in case it is incomplete or lacking some data, you can import that data using the txt, docx, (other supported file format).
