An online retailer selling home goods came to ContextGPT with a familiar problem: support tickets scaling faster than the support team.

## The starting point

Before ContextGPT, the team handled roughly 3,000 tickets a month with a staff of six. Most tickets were repetitive: shipping timelines, return policy, sizing questions.

## What changed

They connected ContextGPT to their product catalog, FAQ pages, and return policy docs. Within two weeks, the chatbot was resolving pre-sales sizing and shipping questions without any human involvement.

```text
Tickets/month before:  3,000
Tickets/month after:     900
Automation rate:          70%
Annual savings:        ~$50,000
```

> [!NOTE]
> Savings came from a combination of reduced headcount growth and faster resolution times — not layoffs. The existing team was reallocated to complex escalations and proactive outreach.

## What made it work

Two things stood out in this case: grounding the chatbot directly in the live product catalog (so answers stayed accurate as inventory changed), and reviewing weekly deflection logs to catch new question patterns before they became ticket spikes.

## Takeaway

The biggest wins came from automating the boring, repetitive 70% — freeing the team to focus on the conversations that actually needed a human.
