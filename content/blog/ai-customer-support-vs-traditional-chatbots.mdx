Traditional chatbots and AI-powered support agents look similar on the surface — both live in a widget on your site. Underneath, they work very differently.

## Rule-based chatbots

Traditional chatbots follow decision trees: "If the user clicks X, show Y." They're predictable but brittle — any question outside the tree gets a dead end or a hand-off to a human.

## AI-powered agents

AI agents like ContextGPT understand natural language and reason over your actual content. They can answer questions nobody explicitly programmed for, because they're grounded in your docs rather than a fixed script.

> [!NOTE]
> "AI-powered" doesn't mean "hallucinating." Retrieval-augmented agents cite the source content they used, so answers stay traceable.

## When rule-based still makes sense

For extremely simple, high-volume flows (e.g., "track my order"), a rule-based button flow can still be faster than a conversation. The best setups often combine both: quick-action buttons for common tasks, AI for everything else.

## Making the switch

Most teams migrate gradually — running the AI agent alongside the old chatbot, then retiring the rule tree once confidence in the AI's answers is high.
