Your chatbot is already talking to every visitor who has a question. With the right setup, it can also qualify them as a lead before a human ever gets involved.

## Ask qualifying questions naturally

Instead of a static form, the chatbot asks qualifying questions as part of the conversation — company size, use case, timeline — only when relevant to what the visitor is asking about.

## Route based on intent

High-intent conversations (pricing questions, "book a demo") can be routed straight to sales, while support questions stay with the AI. Set up routing rules once and let the chatbot handle triage automatically.

```text
Visitor: "What's the pricing for a team of 50?"
→ Routed to: Sales team, tagged "high-intent"
```

> [!TIP]
> Don't gate your best content behind a lead form. Let the chatbot answer freely — the qualifying questions do the capture work without the friction.

## Following up

Every qualified lead should sync straight into your CRM with the full conversation transcript attached, so sales reps start the call already knowing what the prospect asked about.
