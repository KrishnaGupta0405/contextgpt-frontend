Going global with support used to mean hiring native speakers for every market. Modern AI chatbots collapse that cost to nearly zero.

## How language detection works

The chatbot detects the visitor's language from their message — no dropdown, no configuration — and responds in kind, even if your underlying content is only written in English.

## Keep a single source of truth

You don't need to translate your docs into every language. The AI translates on the fly at answer time, which means your content team maintains one canonical version instead of a dozen.

> [!WARNING]
> Machine translation can still get technical or brand-specific terms wrong. Maintain a small glossary of terms that should never be translated (product names, feature names) to keep answers accurate.

## Testing across languages

Spot-check answers in your top 3–5 markets by language, not just English. Tone and idiom often reveal issues that pure translation-quality checks miss.

## Rolling out incrementally

Start with your two or three biggest non-English markets, verify quality, then expand. There's no reason to wait for "full" multilingual coverage before shipping value to your largest segments first.
